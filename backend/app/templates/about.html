Two systems, one plan

This program runs two training systems in parallel: a run periodization engine that ramps volume and intensity toward race day, and an RP-style strength engine (MEV → MAV → MRV → deload) that builds and maintains strength around it. They aren't independent -- the strength mesocycle's deload week is nudged to land near the running plan's own down-weeks and taper, so both systems back off at the same time instead of fighting each other.

The calendar also enforces one guardrail: a hard lower-body strength session shouldn't sit right before a key quality run. When there's no free day to shuffle a session into, the calendar flags the conflict instead of silently letting it slide -- worth a glance, not a blocker.

Why the plan moves

Run paces and HR ceilings autoregulate off real sessions, not just the calendar -- but only within a bounded drift from your profile-set baseline, so a couple of rough weeks can't drag the whole plan off course.

Strength progression works the same way: sets and RIR move based on what you actually log, with the load target computed from your own estimated 1RM rather than a generic percentage table. Both systems are trying to do the same thing -- react to how training is actually going, inside guardrails that keep the plan honest.
